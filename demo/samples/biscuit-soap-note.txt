SUBJECTIVE
Owner reports that Biscuit has been scratching excessively for the past 3 weeks, primarily
affecting the face, paws, axillae and ventral abdomen. Biscuit was previously diagnosed with
atopic dermatitis in June 2022 and has been on intermittent Apoquel therapy since. Owner notes
seasonal exacerbation each spring. Current episode is more severe than previous years; dog is
losing sleep and breaking skin on paws due to self-trauma. No changes to diet, household
cleaners, or bedding. Owner reports head-shaking resolved since last otitis episode in July 2024.

OBJECTIVE
T: 38.4 C  HR: 88 bpm  RR: 22 bpm  Weight: 12.8 kg  BCS: 5/9

Skin examination: Diffuse erythema and lichenification on ventral abdomen, medial thighs and
axillae. Excoriation present on dorsal paws bilaterally. Hyperpigmentation in inguinal fold
consistent with chronic pruritic change. Skin folds moist but no secondary bacterial infection
on cytology — neutrophils absent, Malassezia yeast 2+ on dorsal paw cytology. Ear canals clear
bilaterally. Mild conjunctival erythema OD only.

Intradermal allergy test performed today:
- House dust mite Dermatophagoides pteronyssinus: 3+ wheal (strongly positive)
- Grass pollen mix (5-grass): 3+ wheal (strongly positive)
- Storage mites Tyrophagus putrescentiae: 2+ (moderate positive)
- Cat dander: negative
- Tree pollen mix: 1+ (borderline)
- Full food panel (chicken, beef, wheat, soy, egg, dairy): all negative

ASSESSMENT
1. Atopic Dermatitis — environmental allergens (HDM, grass pollen, storage mites)
   Chronic, recurring condition — first recorded June 2022, predating current policy proposal.
   ICD-10-CM: L20.89 | SNOMED-CT: 24079001 | PRIMARY DIAGNOSIS | PRE-EXISTING CONDITION

2. Allergic Contact Dermatitis — secondary, ventral distribution consistent with contact
   allergen exposure on grass/ground surfaces.
   ICD-10-CM: L23.9 | SNOMED-CT: 40275004

3. Secondary Malassezia dermatitis — mild, self-trauma induced on paws.
   ICD-10-CM: B36.0 | SNOMED-CT: 57569003

PLAN
1. Apoquel (oclacitinib maleate) 16mg SID x30 days. If inadequate response at 2 weeks,
   increase to BID and reassess. Ongoing chronic prescription required.

2. Cytopoint (lokivetmab) 40mg SC administered today. Expected duration 4-8 weeks.
   Schedule repeat injection if clinical signs return.

3. Douxo S3 CALM shampoo — bath twice weekly, leave-on mousse daily to affected areas.
   Avoid over-bathing which can disrupt epidermal barrier.

4. Environmental controls advised: HEPA air purifier in sleeping area, wash bedding at 60 C
   weekly, avoid grass walking during peak pollen season (April-June), wipe paws with damp
   cloth after outdoor walks.

5. Reassess in 6 weeks. If this episode recurs or worsens, initiate allergen-specific
   immunotherapy (ASIT) with the desensitisation protocol from BVSC allergy panel results.

6. Owner fully counselled regarding the chronic, lifelong nature of atopic dermatitis in this
   breed. Advised that this condition and all related treatments (Apoquel, Cytopoint, allergy
   testing, immunotherapy) will be classified as a pre-existing condition under any insurance
   policy incepted after June 2022. Adverse action notice to be issued with policy documents.
